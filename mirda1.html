The Narmada Valley Labs (NVAL) program builds on the cultural skills of geometry and tool-making that are inherent in the children from tribal communities. The NVAL program develops skills in mechatronics (mechanics + electronics) for employment opportunities in the sunrise sector of the Indian economy. This program is conceptualized as a vocational training program for the children from Class 6th to Class 10th taking them through the concepts and applications in programming, engineering, prototyping, design thinking, and aeromodelling to develop a robust base in robotics, artificial intelligence, product design, and science. The course has been designed in partnership with Government, academia, and leading Industry experts.

NVFA strives to harness the latent talent in players to drive sports excellence culture in the region. Our approach towards player development is to build a happy, healthy, and productive life for the players. We have adopted a holistic approach to training and our coaching curriculum inculcates the best of both Dutch and Spanish football methodologies. We use the Coerver method at the beginning phase of training for children to get adept with the ball. Our curriculum mostly derived from the Spanish Football Federation coaching program for U-9 and U-12, especially the rondos whose variations we use on a day-to-day basis. The Dutch method of focusing on technical rather than the tactical at these age groups is highly prioritized too.
